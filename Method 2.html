Feynman Method

The Feynman Method was created by Richard Feynman, a Nobel Prize winner, which uses the tool of teaching. The basic idea of this method is that if you have truly learned something, then you can explain it to different people in different scenarios. This gets rid the need for memorization and makes the person learn and understand the material.

    How to do it?
  • 1. Go over and study the lesson lightly.
  • 2. Pretend to teach the lesson to someone.
  • 3. Identify and review the parts where you lack in.
  • 4. Simplify and repeat it until you have fully understood the lesson.
  • 5. This is optional but you can try to explain and teach it to someone who has a bit of an idea in what the lesson. This ensures that you are explaining the concepts correctly and if there are missing gaps to your knowledge at least there is someone to correct you or add to your knowledge. (Optional)
