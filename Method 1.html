Pomodoro Method

This method was created during the late 1980s by a student named Francesco Cirillo. This method is a great way for studying if you are struggling to focus or feel overwhelmed. The basic gist of the Pomodoro Method is to divide single task into different increments, commonly 25 minutes, which is considered as one session or pomodoro. After one session is done then you get to have a 5 minute break, once you have reached 4 pomodoros you get to have a 30 min. break. This can be done to accomplish a big task or even study for incoming tests or exams.

    How to do it?
  • 1. Know what you want to do/ study.
  • 2. Set a timer for 25 min, this serves as one session/ pomodoro, and focus in studying until the timer rings.
  • 3. Take a 5 min. break.
  • 4. After 4 pomodoros, take a 30 min. break
  • 5. Repeat until the needed task is finished!
