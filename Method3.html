Leitner Method

The basic idea of the Leitner Method, which was created by Sebastian Leitner, is that it applies the concept of spaced repetition. This means that the rather than overloading your brain for in one session, it lets a person learn different concepts over different periods of times. It starts with creating flashcards and studying for the desired lesson and getting 3 small boxes that will fit the flashcard. At first all boxes will go to the first box, then a person starts to answer the different questions on the flashcards. The cards that they get right will go the 2nd box which will be studied only every other day while the cards that the l earner will get wrong retains at the first box which will be reviewed more frequently. After 3 days to a week the next sessions begins, the learner will answer the different flashcards again but this time the cards that they got right from the 2nd box will be transferred to the 3rd box while the cards that were answered wrong will go back to box 1. This process is repeated until all cards are in the 3rd box which signifies that the learner has understood their lesson.

    How to do it?
    • 1. Make flashcards for the desired lesson and get 3 boxes.
    • - 1st box- to be reviewed everyday
    • 2nd box- to be reviewed every other day
    • 3rd box- to be reviewed once a week
  • 2. All cards go to the 1st box at first, then learner starts to answer the different flashcards they prepared.
  • 3. Every card they answered right will move on to the next box while the ones answered wrong will retain or go back to the 1st box.
  • After 3 to 7 days the next sessions begins, the learner will once again answer the questions from the flashcards. Once again every card answered right will move to the next box while the cards answered wrong goes back a box.
  • 5. Then repeat until the lesson is fully understood!
