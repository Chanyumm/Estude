SQ3R Method

This method was proposed by Francis P. Robinson in one of his books called “Effective Study”. He proposed this method because when we are actively reading one specific text this makes learners understand the material better. The name is an abbreviation where S stands for Survey, Q for Question, and the 3Rs being Read, Recite, and Review.

    How to do it?
  • 1. Survey- the first thing that a learner must do to do this method is to read the titles, observe the images, and all in all just read the big texts. This makes sure the student will have an idea in what the material entails and even prepare their mind to read and process the information they are about to read.
  • 2. Question- Before reading the text its self, the learner must first ask different questions just based from the texts they read or images they observed. This ensures that it will make the learner be curious and be eager to learn..
  • 3. Read- After they formulate the different questions, they will now focus to read the material. And as they read the material they should try to answer the questions they formed before and take down note on the important information.ak.
  • 4. Recite- After reading the material, the student needs to recall the information through their own words and either write it down or even record themselves talking about the lesson they just learned.
  • Review- Once they are done recalling the information they just read about, they can now identify where in the lesson may not be as understood which they can correct and go over again. These steps can be repeated over and over again until the learner has fully understood the material.
